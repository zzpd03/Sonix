What is the main purpose of SONIX?

SONIX is a voice-controlled integration system designed to assist individuals with physical disabilities by enabling hands-free operation of computers, communication platforms, and web applications. It ensures accessibility through noise resilience, multilingual support, and cross-platform compatibility.

How was SONIX tested

A usability survey involving more than 40 participants (including individuals with physical disabilities) was conducted. SONIX was evaluated for tasks like web browsing, document editing, video conferencing, and media playback, achieving an average success rate of 80–90% and 95%+ accuracy for multilingual speech recognition

What makes SONIX better than traditional voice assistants?

Unlike traditional voice assistants, SONIX focuses on real-world usability for disabled users by offering better noise resilience, multilingual support, offline capabilities, document handling, and cross-application integration.

Is SONIX capable of functioning without an internet
connection?

Yes, SONIX is designed to support offline functionalities, such as document editing and system-level operations, although certain web-based features like Google searches require internet access.
